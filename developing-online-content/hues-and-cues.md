# The Dealer’s Hand: Advice on Card and Board Games

*Q: What are tips for playing Hues and Cues? Do you have any game recommendations that are similar to it?*

Hello! Great question, I love this game. I’ve played it for two years at multiple family gatherings and with friends, and every time, we’re laughing like crazy.

## What is Hues and Cues?

![Hues and Cues Board Game Box](https://huesandcluesboardgame.com/images/hero-image.png)
Image: Hues and Cues Board Game Box via [huesandcluesboardgame.com](https://huesandcluesboardgame.com/)

Based on the [official website](https://huesandcluesboardgame.com/) for Hues and Cues, it is a party game created by Scott Brady, for ages 8+ and for 3-10 people.

The main goal of the game is to describe a specific color, and hopefully give everyone the best clue you can, because you want them to guess which one you picked. When it's not your turn to give clues, you will be the one doing the guessing.

You can also play this board game [online here](https://huescues.com/).

## Set-up

This is a board game; it comes with a foldable board that is filled with as many shades of colors from the rainbow as you can think of, which mirrors the illustration on the box in the image above.

![Hues and Cues Board](https://huescues.com/full_home_image.png)
Image description: Hues and Cues Board and Cards via [huesandcluesboardgame.com](https://huesandcluesboardgame.com/)

It also comes with small plastic tokens of many colors. Each player should grab a small bag of tokens of the same color, and place one piece at the starting line (top left corner). The gray boxes along the top of the board is the scoring area.

The remaining two pieces will be used to select which colors you think the person is describing with their clues.

## Rules of Play

The person giving the clue will drawing a card (as seen in the second image) that has four color squares on it. They will choose one of those to describe.

There are only two rounds. At the beginning of each round, the person who is describing the color needs to describe it in only one word.

During the second round, you can choose to either abstain and not give another clue, or use two words to describe your color.

Clues cannot include the word the color is, for example, you can't give the clue "orange" even if you use it in the context of it being the name of a fruit.

## Tips for Playing

Very important! Make sure to look at the color you choose (discretely) on the actual physical board. This is because colors often look different to human eyes when surrounded by other similar colors. This often messes new players up.

If everyone is on the right track with their guesses, it is a good idea not to give a second clue, because then they know they should place their second piece in a similar spot (beneficial for both you and them for points!)

For picking your words for your clues, try to think of words as the first image that comes to mind. Because while your clue may be accurate in actuality, if what the majority pictures in their mind doesn't match up, that won't end well.

Another tip for picking words for clues, is being wary of the double-interpretations of your words, and try to avoid words that have equally valid ones if you can. For example, "watermelon" may be tricky, because it could be either pink/red or green (the outside of the fruit). However, my gut reaction to this clue would be to pick pink/red, because that's what I first think of.

Finally, have fun! This is a hard game to be "good at" skill wise unless you are an art major (in which case, rock this as you please). Mainly, it will be filled with laughter over everyone givng "bad clues," some worse than others.

## Game Suggestions Similar to Hues and Cues

The following games are similar because they involve guessing what something is based on clue words.

• Charades. The classic game where you act out a word using your body, or create scenes with your teammates.
• Heads Up! App Game. This is an app that facilitates charades through an app interface, through categories.

• [HedBanz Board Game](https://board-games.fandom.com/wiki/Hedbanz). This game makes you wear headbands that hold up your designated word.
