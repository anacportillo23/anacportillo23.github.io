Hispanic Influence and Family Storytelling: Poems

These are influential poems I have read, some written in Spanish and some in English that are not of Hispanic experiences, but discusses POC themes, such as not fitting in due to our culture, nonetheless.

1. "My Name is Hiwot" by Hiwot Adilow

This was the first young POC poet I ever saw, and the first time a pom really spoke to my experience as a Latina in a predominantly white area. I also really admire the way she performs anger. It gave me chills and made me cry the first time I heard it.

Watch on YouTube

2. "Puetro Rican Obituary” by Pedro Pietri

This was the first Hispanic poem I ever read. It introduced me to anaphoras, which is when you repeat a phrase over and over again in a poem. A line that really moved me was “Assistant Assistant Lavaplatos” because it recognized the inequality of Latinos not being able to move up in job titles at all. It inspired me to actually have confidence in myself to go for higher positions at my magazine.

Watch on YouTube Read the Poem Here

3. "Los Pobres” by Pedro Pietri

One of my dad’s favorites. I carry on my dad’s favorites in poetry as well as music too, which is apparant throughout this. When I read it for the first time, I read it aloud in Spanish and tried to translate it for myself, while he watched. It was very moving for me.
